1.3 Field Lines

Many students have never actually written a program to draw field lines. In doing so, you‚Äôll gain a bit of insight into how the field line drawings you see in books are created. In addition, you‚Äôll appreciate some of the limitations of field lines. In the development of the theory of electricity and magnetism, the concept of a field line was central. (They were introduced by Faraday and Maxwell referred to and used them extensively.) In addition, the textbook emphasizes field lines and ‚Äútubes of flux‚Äù.

Background

Consider a 2-D vector field defined by

F=Fx‚Äã(x,y)x^+Fy‚Äã(x,y)y^‚Äã

A field line is a line that is always tangent to the vector F.

To determine the equations that must be solved to find the field line, consider a differential distance ds drawn in the direction of F at a given point. This step has a horizontal component of dx and a vertical component of dy.

The differential triangle is similar to a triangle formed with a Fx‚Äã in the x-direction, Fy‚Äã in the y-direction, and F as the hypotenuse so that

dsdx‚Äã=F(x,y)Fx‚Äã(x,y)‚Äã

dsdy‚Äã=F(x,y)Fy‚Äã(x,y)‚Äã

where F(x,y)=Fx2‚Äã+Fy2‚Äã‚Äã.

We have now formulated the statement ‚Äúa field line is a line that is always tangent to the vector F(x,y)‚Äù as a set of two differential equations. The field line corresponds to the solution for x(s) and y(s) and the quantity s corresponds to the length of the field line.

To compute x(s) and y(s), we can solve the above equations in the same way that differential equations for the trajectory of a particle are solved. Using the Forward Euler approximation

dsdx‚Äã‚âÉŒîsx(s+Œîs)‚àíx(s)‚Äã

dsdy‚Äã‚âÉŒîsy(s+Œîs)‚àíy(s)‚Äã

gives

x(s+Œîs)=x(s)+ŒîsF(x(s),y(s))Fx‚Äã(x(s),y(s))‚Äã

y(s+Œîs)=y(s)+ŒîsF(x(s),y(s))Fy‚Äã(x(s),y(s))‚Äã

To find the field line, we must specify initial positions x(0) and y(0) and also a step size Œîs and then iterate the above two equations. Discretized, the above two equations are

xi+1‚Äã=xi‚Äã+ŒîsF(xi‚Äã,yi‚Äã)Fx‚Äã(xi‚Äã,yi‚Äã)‚Äã

yi+1‚Äã=yi‚Äã+ŒîsF(xi‚Äã,yi‚Äã)Fy‚Äã(xi‚Äã,yi‚Äã)‚Äã
